Research

Four directions in audio, AI, and perception.

My work spans machine listening, trustworthy AI, multimodal systems, and interdisciplinary applications. Below is an overview of each research direction.

01

Audio and Music Intelligence

Research on acoustic scene and event analysis, audio tagging and captioning, music information retrieval and symbolic music generation, bioacoustics and healthcare audio, and speech and voice enhancement. Core methods include deep learning, self-supervised representation, and transfer learning applied to diverse audio signals.

  • Acoustic scene analysis
  • Music information retrieval
  • Bioacoustics
  • Speech enhancement
02

Robust, Trustworthy and Efficient AI

Investigating domain adaptation and domain shift, few-shot and data-efficient learning, model pruning and efficiency, source tracing and privacy, and audio security including adversarial attacks and spoofing detection. The goal is to build AI systems that are reliable, fair, and deployable under real-world constraints.

  • Domain adaptation
  • Few-shot learning
  • Model efficiency
  • Audio security
03

Multimodal and Cross-Domain Perception

Exploring audio-visual captioning and retrieval, multimodal scene classification, sign language and skeleton-based perception, indoor positioning and radar sensing, and audio question answering with modality importance analysis. This direction bridges audio, vision, and sensor data for richer environmental understanding.

  • Audio-visual learning
  • Multimodal classification
  • Sign language recognition
  • Radar sensing
04

Peripheral Review and Interdisciplinary Works

Survey and review work on human-centred computer audition, alongside interdisciplinary research in AI education assessment, optical communication signal processing, digital audio media, and hardware FPGA systems. These projects connect machine listening to education, communications, and embedded systems.

  • Computer audition review
  • AI education
  • Signal processing
  • FPGA systems
